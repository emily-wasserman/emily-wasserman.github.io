Structure in the space of
moral representations


There are many, and many different, ways to do something wrong. Is there structure in the space of harmful acts?

I used representational similarity analysis, PCA, and K-means clustering to model where, across the brain, different features drive convergence among representations of these acts.

Harmful acts committed
by the self and others


When we do something bad, we often let ourselves off the hook - but not always. Past data shows that people condemn themselves slightly more than others for accidental harms.

I'm leading an fMRI project to discover how representations of our own and others' minds contribute to this effect.

Altruism in the brain


Imagine helping a person in need. In your mind, are you picturing just a person - or a whole scene?

As a collaborator, I ran mixed linear models in R on fMRI and behavioral data to investigate how activity in brain regions that represent information about scenes contributes to our decisions to help others.
